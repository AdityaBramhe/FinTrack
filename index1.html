Financial Tips

The 50/30/20 Rule

Allocate 50% of your income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings. This simple budgeting method can help maintain financial balance and ensure you're saving adequately while still enjoying life.

Emergency Fund First

Before investing or paying off low-interest debt, build an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of expenses. This financial safety net will help you handle unexpected costs without derailing your financial progress.

Pay Yourself First

Treat savings as a non-negotiable expense by automatically transferring a portion of your income to savings accounts before spending on anything else. This habit ensures consistent saving regardless of other expenses.

Track Every Expense

Regularly monitoring where your money goes makes you more conscious of spending habits and helps identify unnecessary expenses. Use this app to categorize and review your spending patterns monthly.

Avoid Lifestyle Inflation

When your income increases, avoid proportionally increasing your spending. Instead, allocate the additional income to savings and investments while maintaining your current lifestyle.
